## B-roll / screen-recording checklist

Capture these before you edit — tick as you go:

- [ ] **Real Lola** — 2–3 cute clips (looking at camera, a little head tilt, lying by your desk).
- [ ] **Desktop-Lola floating** — clean screen recording of her in the corner while you "work."
- [ ] **You at your desk** — wide shot, screen-heavy, natural.
- [ ] **Uchinoko Maker** — screen-rec of assembling Lola (body → face → bandana). Slow, deliberate.
- [ ] **Claude Code moment** — screen-rec of dropping Lola's image in → her turning into vector art.
- [ ] **Typing her lines** — screen-rec of you writing her sentences / personality.
- [ ] **Open/close** — click menu-bar 🐶 (pops in) and × (tucks away). Do it twice, crisp.
- [ ] **The tap** — click desktop-Lola → her bounce + "hi!" speech bubble.
- [ ] **Side-by-side ender** — real Lola + desktop-Lola in one frame.

## Editing recipe

- **Aspect:** 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920.
- **Captions:** burn them in — most viewers are on mute. Keep the bold on-screen lines from `script.md`.
- **Pace:** cut every 2–3s through THE BUILD so it never feels like a lecture.
- **Music:** soft kawaii lo-fi, low under the VO.
- **SFX:** a small "pop" on the bounce (~0:42).
- **End-card:** reuse the same 2-sec series card every episode (see `_series-kit/`).

## Suggested tools

- **Record:** Screen Studio (Mac, auto-zoom) or QuickTime.
- **Edit + auto-caption:** CapCut.
- **Thumbnail:** Figma (`_series-kit/thumbnail-template.fig`).
